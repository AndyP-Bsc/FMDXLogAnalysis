FM DX Interactive Log Analysis - Help File

Purpose

The purpose of this application is to provide an online interactive statistical analysis tool using data from my own FM DX loggings from the years 2014 and 2015

FM DX

FM DX is the hobby of receiving FM radio station well beyond their intended geographical target area by harnessing different types of radio propagation events

Using the Application

There are 3 simple controls:

  1. Year: Select All Years, 2014 or 2015 specific logbook data
  2. Month: Select All Months or a Specific Month of logbook data
  3. Signal Propagation Mode: Select All radio propagation enhancement modes or a specific mode to analyse

These controls then effect the bar plot on the right which illustrates the total number of FM DX logs made over the Months or Days

Below the bar plot are calculations that show the minimum, maximum and average distance (in miles) between the radio receiver and transmitter

What are propagation modes?

For those not familiar with the different types of radio propagation:

  • Aurora: The solar wind (from the Sun) hitting the Earth's atmosphere ionises the aurora curtain 'sometimes' causing radio signals to be reflected beyond their target area
  • Meteor Scatter: This is where meteor's burning up in the atmosphere leave a trail of ionised particles which reflect radio signal's beyond their target area
  • Sporadic E: Sporadic E refers to the sporadic nature of this type of radio propagation. E refers to the E layer of the atmosphere which reflects signals well beyond their target area
  • Tropospheric: This is where radio signals follow a duct between warm and cold air extending the normal radio range
